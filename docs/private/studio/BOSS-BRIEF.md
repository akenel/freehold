> ## The ask
> **Approve a 2-week, zero-build validation sprint.** No product code, no spend beyond
> the team's time. At the end you get a signed go/no-go with real numbers. If it's
> "go," we build; if not, we've spent two weeks instead of three months finding out.

---

## The opportunity

Governments are turning **compliance into a permanent tax on small firms** — and the
smallest can't afford enterprise tooling. Germany just put a **hard deadline** on it:
**every trades firm must issue digital invoices by 1 January 2028.** That's **~1 million
German Handwerk firms, averaging 6 people** — forced to act, and too small for the
heavyweight tools. *(Sourced, fact-checked: German Federal Ministry of Finance / IHK / DATEV.)*

## Why us, why now

- **We know the customer** — construction/trades is our domain and our language.
- **We land at home first.** Switzerland has its own dated trigger (the QR-bill bank
  cutoff, **30 Sept 2026**), so we get **paying local users, low risk**, before touching Germany.
- **Our infrastructure is already built** (the Freehold starter kit) — the team builds
  *product*, not plumbing.
- **The moat is local:** Swiss multilingual (DE/FR/IT) + data-residency repels the big
  foreign players; the German giants are too complex for a two-person crew.

## The plan

| | Beachhead | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Where** | Switzerland (home) | Germany |
| **Trigger** | QR-bill cutoff, Sept 2026 | E-invoice mandate, Jan 2028 |
| **Product** | Small, dated invoicing fixer | The "life raft" — job→invoice workflow for tiny crews |
| **Goal** | First revenue, trust | The 1M-firm market |

## What could kill this — and how we check it cheaply (the honest part)

I am not going to pretend this is a sure thing. Three things could sink it, and the
2-week sprint tests each **before** we commit budget:

1. **The invoice format is already commoditised.** Our edge must be a *simpler workflow*
   for tiny crews, not the format. → We trial the competitors; if they're already
   simple enough, **we stop.**
2. **Will they actually pay?** Every pricing figure we found failed our fact-check. → We
   interview 10 real firms about what they *already spend*, not what they'd "like."
3. **The accountant may control the sale.** → We ask every firm who chooses their software.

## The decision I need from you

**Green-light the 2-week sprint.** It costs nothing but the team's time, it de-risks the
biggest unknowns, and it tells us — with evidence, not opinion — whether this is worth
building. That's the responsible way to bet a junior team.
