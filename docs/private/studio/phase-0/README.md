# Phase 0 · Validate before we build

*Two weeks. No product code. The job of this phase is to answer five questions
honestly enough that the go/no-go decision defends itself — and to catch the one way
this whole thesis could be dead on arrival.*

This pack turns [../03-roadmap.md](../03-roadmap.md) Phase 0 into something a junior
pair can run on Monday.

## Why this phase exists (say this to the boss)

In [01-pain-landscape](../01-pain-landscape.md) we proved the **regulation** is real
(Germany's 2028 e-invoicing deadline; the Swiss forcing-function cluster —
primary-sourced). What we did **not** prove is the part that decides whether there's a
*business*: that small firms will **pay us**, and that the invoice *workflow* for tiny
crews is genuinely underserved. Every pricing / adoption / competitor-gap claim in the
research **failed** fact-checking. Two weeks buys that certainty before three months of
build. A new lead who validates first is one who doesn't get burned.

## The gate — TWO tests, both must pass

> **Test 1 — Demand:** ≥ **6 of 10** Swiss firms show real invoicing/compliance pain
> **and** already spend money on it today (software, bookkeeper, or their own hours) —
> a budget we can redirect, not just a complaint.
>
> **Test 2 — The gap is real:** the teardown ([competitor-teardown](competitor-teardown.md))
> finds a *genuine workflow gap* for tiny crews — something plancraft / ToolTime / TAIFUN
> make painful that we could make simple. **The e-invoice format is commoditized; if we
> can't beat them on workflow, there is no wedge.**

**If Test 2 fails, STOP.** That's the whole reason this phase is cheap and fast: better
to kill a dead thesis with free trials than with a failed launch.

## Two-week shape

| Days | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Recruit 10 Swiss firms (your own network — you're *in* construction) + line up trials. |
| 3–8 | Swiss interviews (WS1) + competitor teardown (WS3) in parallel. |
| 4–9 | German Handwerk outreach (WS2) + format/channel digging (WS4) alongside. |
| 9–10 | Fill the scorecard (WS5). Make the call. Write the boss brief. |

## Rules

1. **No pitching.** Describe the idea and the answers turn to polite lies. Ask about their world.
2. **Log everything the same day.** Memory rewrites interviews to match your hopes.
3. **Chase existing spend and past behaviour**, never "would you…". Intentions are free and worthless.
4. **One place for findings** — the [decision-scorecard](decision-scorecard.md).
