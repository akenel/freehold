# Phase 0 · Validate before we build

*Two weeks. No product code. The single job of this phase is to answer four
questions honestly enough that the go/no-go decision defends itself.*

This pack turns [../03-roadmap.md](../03-roadmap.md) Phase 0 into something a junior
pair can actually run on Monday morning.

## Why this phase exists (say this to the boss)

In [01-pain-landscape](../01-pain-landscape.md) we proved the **regulation** is real
(CIS reforms, MTD, Peppol — primary-sourced). What we did **not** prove is that small
firms will **pay us** to help with it. Every pricing / willingness-to-pay claim we
found *failed* fact-checking. So we spend two weeks buying certainty **before** we
spend three months building. A new lead who validates first is a new lead who doesn't
get burned.

## The four workstreams

| # | Question | Doc | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Is the CIS pain real, and will they pay? | [interview-guide.md](interview-guide.md) | Lead + 1 junior | 10 scored interviews |
| 2 | Where do incumbents actually fail small crews? | [competitor-teardown.md](competitor-teardown.md) | Junior pair | Gap memo |
| 3 | What does it cost/take to be allowed to sell this? | [certification-brief.md](certification-brief.md) | Lead | 1-page cost/time answer |
| 4 | Does the whole thing add up to go/no-go? | [decision-scorecard.md](decision-scorecard.md) | Lead | Signed decision |

## The gate (decide this, don't drift past it)

> **GO if:** ≥ **6 of 10** interviewed firms describe the CIS burden as real **and**
> already spend money (accountant fees, software, or their own hours) on it today —
> i.e. there is a **budget we can redirect**, not just a complaint.
>
> **NO-GO / rethink if:** most firms shrug it off, hand it entirely to an accountant
> they're happy with, or show no existing spend to capture.

The gate is about **existing spend**, not enthusiasm. People are generous with "yes";
they are honest with their wallets. We measure the wallet.

## Two-week shape

| Days | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Recruit 10 firms (start with the company's own network — we're *in* construction). Set up the trials. |
| 3–8 | Run interviews (workstream 1) + competitor teardown (2) in parallel. |
| 4–9 | Certification digging (3) — runs alongside; it's reading + a couple of calls/emails. |
| 9–10 | Fill the scorecard (4). Make the call. Write it up for the boss. |

## Rules for this phase

1. **No pitching.** The second you describe our idea, the answers become worthless.
   Ask about their world; listen. (See the interview guide's "traps.")
2. **Log everything the same day.** Memory rewrites interviews to match your hopes.
3. **Chase existing spend and past behaviour**, never "would you…". Future intentions
   are free to give and worth nothing.
4. **One place for findings** — the [decision-scorecard](decision-scorecard.md). If it
   isn't written down there, it didn't happen.
