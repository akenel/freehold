## The core thesis

**The easiest software to sell is software a business is legally required to own.**
For UK/EU small firms — and construction especially — a wall of regulation lands
between 2026 and 2030 that turns "nice to have" into "must file or get penalised."
That is our wedge: **compliance-driven forced-buy demand**, in the sector we know.

Demand being *compelled by law* is the part we can prove cold. What we can't yet
prove — pricing, exact competitor gaps — is flagged honestly in §5.

## 1. The regulatory wall (this is the strong ground)

### ✅ UK Making Tax Digital (MTD) for Income Tax — *live now*
- From **6 April 2026**, sole traders & landlords with qualifying income **over
  £50,000** must keep digital records and file **quarterly** using
  **HMRC-recognised software**. You **cannot file manually**.
- Threshold drops to **£30,000 (Apr 2027)** and **£20,000 (Apr 2028)** — the net
  widens every year.
- First cohort ≈ **780,000** taxpayers; a further ≈ **970,000** from 2027.
- *Source: GOV.UK MTD guidance; Avalara 2026 guide.* [1][6]

### ✅ Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) reforms — *live April 2026, construction-specific*
This is the one that sits right on our domain.
- **Reinstated mandatory monthly returns** — you must file **even with zero
  subcontractors paid** (a nil return), or notify HMRC of inactivity in advance.
- **Mandatory subcontractor verification** *plus* demonstrable **ongoing
  monitoring** of engagements — a continuous record-keeping duty, not a one-off.
- **"Knew or should have known" supply-chain liability** — a contractor can be on
  the hook for fraud down its chain.
- Enforced by a **£1.7bn HMRC investment**, **5,500 new compliance staff**, and
  **AI/automated cross-referencing** that now flags the three classic CIS error
  modes (unreported subcontractors, wrong labour/materials splits, column errors).
- *Sources: Simply Business; Johnston Carmichael; Hudson Contract; GOV.UK Spending Review 2025.* [2][3][4]
- ⚠️ *Do not quote the specific "15–30% penalty / 4-year recovery" figure — that
  claim failed fact-checking. The obligations above are solid; that penalty number isn't.*

### ✅ E-invoicing: UK Peppol (2029) + EU ViDA (now → 2030) — *the long, durable wave*
- **UK**: government confirmed **Peppol** as the core network toward a **B2B/B2G
  e-invoicing mandate from April 2029** (phased, biggest firms first). Detailed
  roadmap expected at the **Nov 2026 Budget** — so scope may still shift.
- **EU**: the **ViDA** package is **in force since 14 April 2025**. It already lets
  member states **compel domestic e-invoicing without EU sign-off** (Germany 2025,
  France 2026 are proceeding), and mandates **structured cross-border B2B
  e-invoicing + near-real-time digital reporting from 1 July 2030**.
- *Sources: GOV.UK Tax Update 2026; European Commission ViDA page.* [5][7]

### 🟡 Building Safety Act 2022 — "golden thread"
- A **live legal duty** (Section 88, commenced 1 Oct 2023, detailed by SI 2024/41):
  accountable persons must keep prescribed structured info on **Higher-Risk
  Buildings**. It's a document-management compliance obligation.
- **But scope is narrow** — only Higher-Risk Buildings — so the forced-buy
  population is *far smaller* than CIS or MTD. Interesting, not the wedge.
- *Source: Fire Safety Event (vendor-adjacent) + legislation.gov.uk. One dissenting reviewer.* [8]

## 2. The market, by the numbers (✅ all primary-sourced)

### United Kingdom
- **5.7m** private-sector businesses: **4.27m** non-employing, **1.15m**
  employer-micro, **~220,000** small firms (10–49 employees).
- **Construction is the single largest SME sector: 885,000 firms (16%).**
- The founder's **3–50 employee** target band ≈ low hundreds of thousands of firms,
  heavily construction-weighted.
- *Source: GOV.UK / DBT Business Population Estimates 2025.* [9]

### European Union (the expansion market as ViDA rolls out)
- **32.3m enterprises** (2022), **99% micro/small (0–49 staff)**, employing
  **77.5m** people (48% of all enterprise employment).
- **Construction alone: 12% of EU enterprises, €2.1tn turnover, 13.8m workers.**
- *Source: Eurostat Structural Business Statistics, Oct 2024.* [10]

**Read:** the buyer we understand best (small construction firms) is simultaneously
the **largest** SME sector *and* the one getting hit **hardest and earliest** by new
compliance duties. That alignment is the opportunity.

## 3. Segmented view

### By business size
| Segment | Size | Compliance reality | Software posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Micro / solo** | 1–3 | MTD hits them as thresholds fall to £20k; CIS if they engage subs | Price-sensitive, phone-first, allergic to complexity. Wants *one button*. |
| **Small** | 4–15 | CIS monthly returns + verification bite hardest here; multiple jobs, real subs | **The sweet spot.** Enough pain to pay, too small for enterprise tools. |
| **Small-medium** | 16–50 | Full CIS monitoring burden, approaching Peppol scope | Will pay real money; may already have Xero/Sage to integrate with, not replace. |

### By sector (priority order)
1. **Construction & trades** — our domain, biggest UK SME sector, earliest + heaviest compliance load (CIS + MTD + reverse-charge VAT + eventually Peppol). **Start here.**
2. **Professional services** — MTD + e-invoicing exposure, but crowded by accounting incumbents.
3. **Retail / hospitality** — e-invoicing exposure later; different pain shape (POS, rotas).
4. **Logistics** — e-invoicing + cross-border ViDA reporting; more complex, later.

## 4. The incumbents (⚠️ indicative — treat as leads, not gospel)

The competitor and pricing detail below came through in research **but did not
survive strict fact-checking** — the pricing pages are real, but we haven't
independently confirmed them. Use as a **map of who's there**, and go verify before
quoting to the boss.

- **Trade job-management tools**: Tradify (~£34/user/mo, reported), Commusoft
  (aimed at 5+ engineer teams), Jobber, Powered Now (~£19–27/user/mo, reported),
  iTrade (~£29/field worker/mo, admins free — reported).
- **Accounting / MTD**: Xero, QuickBooks, Sage, FreeAgent, FreshBooks — big, funded, already MTD-recognised.
- **Reported pricing shape** for small crews: sole traders **£15–30**, small teams
  **£30–50**, enterprise **£80+** per user/month. *(Unverified — validate.)*
- **The gap people mention** (unverified, but the most promising lead): general trade
  tools and general accounting tools are **weak on UK-specific construction
  compliance — CIS and the Domestic Reverse Charge VAT** — and **per-user pricing
  scales badly for teams of 10+**. If true, that's precisely our opening.

## 5. What we DON'T yet know (put this in front of the boss yourself)

Leading with the unknowns is how a new lead earns trust. These four gaps are the
homework before we commit build budget — see [03-roadmap.md](03-roadmap.md) Phase 0.

1. **Willingness-to-pay / real price points** — every pricing/WTP/adoption claim we
   found was *refuted* on inspection. We have **not** proven what a small crew will
   actually pay. **Validate with real customers first.**
2. **The specific incumbent gap** — the "nobody does CIS + reverse-charge well for
   small teams" story is a *hypothesis*, not a confirmed finding. Confirm by
   hands-on testing of Tradify/Commusoft/Xero.
3. **The certification barrier (this one is critical).** To sell **MTD** or
   **e-invoicing** software you need **HMRC recognition** and/or **Peppol Access
   Point certification** — cost, process, and lead time **unknown**. This may make
   pure filing tools too heavy for a first product. See how we route around it in
   [02-app-portfolio.md](02-app-portfolio.md).
4. **Self-host vs hosted expectation** — non-technical small firms expect
   fully-hosted SaaS. Freehold *can* self-host, but whether that's an asset or a
   distraction for this buyer is unproven. (It's likely a *back-office* advantage
   for us — cheap, ownable infra — not a customer-facing selling point.)
